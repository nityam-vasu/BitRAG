# THE UNKNOWN - Character Profiles & Backstories

---

## DR. ELENA VANCE - The Protagonist

### Background

Dr. Elena Vance stands at thirty-two as one of the most promising psychiatrists in Boston, though her gender has made every achievement an uphill battle. She graduated valedictorian from Johns Hopkins Medical School in 1919, a time when women in medicine were still viewed as novelties rather than professionals. Her dissertation on traumatic memory—"The Ghosts We Carry Within"—was revolutionary for its era, arguing that patients' unexplained physical symptoms often stemmed from suppressed psychological trauma rather than purely physiological causes.

But Elena's interest in the mind goes beyond academic curiosity. At twelve years old, she witnessed her mother, Catherine Vance, walk into the fog-shrouded garden of their Boston home and simply vanish. No footprints, no trace, no explanation. The police called it a runaway case; Elena knew better. Her mother would never have left without her locket—the silver piece Elena still wears around her neck to this day.

### The Locket

The locket is Elena's most treasured possession and her greatest mystery. Inside is a faded photograph of her mother, but the image is strange—half of Catherine's face is clear, the other half seems to shimmer and shift when viewed directly. Elena has spent twenty years studying that impossible photograph, trying to understand what it means.

### Her Practice

Elena runs a small but respected practice in Boston's Beacon Hill neighborhood. She specializes in patients other doctors have given up on—women diagnosed with "hysterical" conditions, trauma survivors dismissed as imaginative, and those whose physical ailments defied medical explanation. Her methods are considered unconventional: she uses hypnosis, dream analysis, and what she calls "memory excavation"—guiding patients to confront suppressed trauma.

This expertise is exactly what drew the attention of the Blackwood family. When their letter arrived, mentioning her uncle Marcus and offering answers about her mother, Elena felt the pull of a case she couldn't refuse.

### Psychological Profile

Elena is brilliant, driven, and has a stubborn streak that borders on self-destruction. She trusts her instincts over conventional wisdom and has a talent for seeing patterns others miss. However, her professional detachment can slip when her personal emotions are involved—her mother's disappearance has left her with a deep fear of losing control, which she masks with intellectual precision.

Her greatest strength is also her vulnerability: she believes that every mystery has an answer, every puzzle can be solved. This faith will be tested by what she discovers in Ravenshollow.

## THOMAS BLACKWOOD - The Patriarch

### Background

At fifty-eight, Thomas Blackwood carries the weight of three generations of family secrets on his shoulders. He inherited the Blackwood estate at twenty-three when his father, Nathaniel Blackwood, suffered a mental collapse that left him institutionalized until his death. Thomas has maintained the family's crumbling legacy ever since, keeping the dark truths buried beneath polite Victorian propriety.

Thomas was never meant to be the head of the family. That role belonged to his younger brother, Marcus—the brilliant one, the visionary. But Marcus's obsession with the family's hidden history eventually drove him to isolate himself in Blackwood Manor, spending his final years in increasingly strange research. Thomas tried to intervene, to protect the family name, but Marcus would not be stopped.

### His Role in the Mystery

Thomas knows more about The Watcher than he will ever admit. He has seen it—glimpsed it in mirrors, felt its presence in the hallways at night. But he has also learned that ignorance is a form of survival. The Blackwood family has bargained with the entity for generations; Thomas's role has been to pay the price without asking questions.

When Marcus died, Thomas saw an opportunity to end the cycle—or at least delay it. The letter to Elena was his gamble: perhaps Marcus's niece, born under such unusual circumstances, might finally provide the answers they needed.

### The Secret He Keeps

Thomas knows that Elena is not simply Marcus's niece. He was present in 1894 when Catherine Vance arrived at Blackwood Manor, pregnant and fleeing something she couldn't name. He watched as the ritual was performed—the one that would give the child a connection to The Watcher, a bridge between worlds. He saw the fog roll in, heard the screams that lasted seven days, and when it finally cleared, Catherine was gone but a baby remained.

Elena is not Marcus's niece. She is something else entirely—something the Blackwoods created.

## ADELAIDE BLACKWOOD - The Daughter

### Background

At twenty-six, Adelaide Blackwood has lived her entire life in the shadow of Blackwood Manor. Her mother died giving birth to her—a fact Thomas never lets Adelaide forget. She grew up believing she was cursed, that her very existence had caused her mother's death.

Adelaide is beautiful in an ethereal way, with dark hair and eyes that seem to hold secrets. But her beauty is tinged with something haunting; she rarely sleeps well, and her dreams are filled with visions she cannot control.

### Her Connection to The Watcher

Adelaide has seen The Watcher since childhood. Not as a terrifying monster, but as a figure that stands at the edge of her vision—always present, always watching. She learned early not to speak of it; her father's reaction to her childhood drawings of "the man with no face" taught her that some truths are too dangerous to voice.

But Adelaide has also learned something else: The Watcher is not her enemy. It is waiting, she believes, for someone specific. Someone who can finally free it—and her—from the bargain that has bound their family for generations.

### Her Secret

Adelaide has been communicating with The Watcher through her dreams. It has shown her things: the truth about 1894, the role Elena's mother played, and the fact that Elena's arrival was foretold. Adelaide believes Elena is the key—but she is afraid to trust anyone, even her own family. Her cryptic warnings are her way of trying to help without directly involving herself.

## DR. MARCUS BLACKWOOD - The Deceased

### Background

Dr. Marcus Blackwood (1852-1924) was the black sheep of the family—a brilliant scholar who pursued forbidden knowledge with obsessive dedication. He studied at Cambridge and Heidelberg, specializing in occult anthropology and comparative religion. But his true research was far more dangerous: he sought to understand The Watcher, the entity that had haunted the Blackwood family since their arrival in Massachusetts in 1664.

Marcus spent decades documenting the family's history, piecing together the truth from fragmented journals, whispered legends, and his own experiments. By 1920, he believed he had finally understood the entity—and how to communicate with it.

### His Research

Marcus's final work, "The Entity Between: A Study in Liminal Existence," was never published. In it, he argued that The Watcher is not a demon or spirit but something older and stranger: a being that exists in the mathematical space between states of matter, between moments of time, between consciousness and void. It is neither alive nor dead, neither present nor absent—it simply is.

Marcus believed that with the right "bridge"—a human consciousness trained to perceive beyond normal reality—he could finally communicate with The Watcher and perhaps even bargain for the family's freedom.

### His Death

Marcus died in the manor's library, a room he had sealed and warded with symbols from his research. The door was locked from the inside; his body showed no signs of violence. The official cause of death was heart failure, but those who saw his face knew better. Marcus had seen something in that room—something that finally answered his questions.

His final letter to Elena contained only a key and these words: "The answers you seek are not in the past, child. They are in what happens next."

## CAPTAIN HAROLD CROSBY - The Witness

### Background

At sixty-seven, Harold Crosby has spent half a century trying to forget what he saw in September 1894. As a young man of seventeen, he was the sole survivor of the event that claimed seven lives in a single week—the Blackwood Festival, as it was called, a gathering of the town's elite that ended in tragedy and mystery.

Harold never spoke of what he witnessed—the fog rolling in from the sea, the sounds that seemed to come from everywhere and nowhere, the faces of his friends as they were taken. But he never stopped watching Blackwood Manor either, waiting for something he couldn't name.

### What He Knows

Harold is the only living person who can provide firsthand testimony about 1894. He knows about Catherine Vance and the strange ritual that preceded the disappearances. He knows that Elena was born from that ritual—though he doesn't know her true identity. And he knows that The Watcher is real, that it took his friends, and that it is still waiting.

### His Role

Harold becomes Elena's unlikely ally, providing her with pieces of the puzzle that Thomas would prefer remain hidden. But his help comes with a price: he wants Elena to finish what was started in 1894, to finally free the souls that were taken.

## DR. MARGARET HOLLOWAY - The Rival

### Background

Dr. Margaret Holloway (born 1881) is everything Elena aspires to be—and everything she fears becoming. A prominent psychiatrist at Boston General, Margaret has built her career on dismissing "feminine weakness" in her patients while suffering from her own private torments. She and Elena were once colleagues, before Margaret's jealousy over Elena's innovative methods created an irreconcilable rift.

### Her Secret Agenda

Margaret came to Ravenshollow for the same reason as Elena—to investigate Marcus Blackwood's research—but for entirely different purposes. She believes that understanding The Watcher could revolutionize psychology, that the entity's ability to manipulate perception could be harnessed for therapeutic purposes. She is willing to do whatever it takes to obtain Marcus's notes.

Her rivalry with Elena is both professional and personal; she wants to prove that her methods are superior, that she is not "hysterical" like the patients she dismisses. But her ambition may lead her to make a deal that puts everyone in danger.

## THE WATCHER - The Entity

### Nature

The Watcher defies simple categorization. It is not a ghost, demon, or alien—it is something that exists outside human understanding. It perceives reality not as humans do, but as a continuous flow of states, able to see all possible outcomes simultaneously. It does not think, feel, or want in human terms—it simply observes, waiting for the moment when its observation can finally become interaction.

### The Bargain

For over three centuries, the Blackwood family has maintained a bargain with The Watcher: protection in exchange for... something. The exact terms have been lost to time, but Marcus discovered the truth in his research. The Watcher does not want souls or suffering—it wants to be understood. It wants a bridge, a human consciousness that can perceive it directly. The Blackwoods have been providing that bridge, one generation at a time, without knowing exactly why.

### The Truth About Elena

Elena is the final product of the original bargain. Born during the ritual of 1894, she was conceived specifically to be the perfect bridge—a consciousness that could exist partially in The Watcher's realm. Her mother's disappearance was not a tragedy but a translation: Catherine chose to become part of The Watcher, to exist in the between, giving her daughter the gift of perception at a terrible cost.

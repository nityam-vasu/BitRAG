# THE UNKNOWN - Chapter One (Indian Context)

## The Arrival

The train whistle screamed like something wounded as it pulled into Kodalipur Station, a mournful sound that seemed to echo off the surrounding hills and return multiplied. Dr. Aisha Sharma pressed her palm against the grimy window, watching the small coastal village emerge from the October mist like a ghost taking shape from mist.

Kodalipur. She hadn't spoken the name in twenty years, hadn't allowed herself to think it except in her darkest moments. And now here she was, returning for the first time since she was twelve years old, summoned by a letter from a dead man.

The letter had arrived three days ago, bearing the Ravanhalli family seal. Inside, a single key and a message: "Your uncle Marcus has died. He left something for you. Come before the mist of Kartik, or it will be too late. Come alone."

Aisha had read those words a hundred times. The handwriting was not Marcus's, but the key was real enough. And so was the return address: Ravanhalli Manor.

The train ground to a halt with a shudder that rattled Aisha's teeth. Through the window, she could see the platform: empty except for a single figure standing beneath the amber glow of an oil lamp. The figure was draped in white, face obscured by the mist that rolled in from somewhere beyond the station building.

"Dr. Sharma?" The voice was male, aged, careful. "I've been sent to collect you."

---

## The Road to Ravanhalli Manor

The journey took nearly an hour. The road wound along the cliff edge, and Aisha could hear the waves crashing against the rocks below. Twice, her guide—a man named Jeremiah—turned to look at her, his face pale in the carriage lantern's glow.

"Your uncle spoke of you often," he said finally, as the carriage crested a hill and the first glimpse of Ravanhalli Manor appeared through the mist.

"I never knew my uncle Marcus. He was my mother's brother, wasn't he?"

Jeremiah's hands tightened on the reins. "He was. A brilliant man. Too brilliant, perhaps. The things he found in the manor's depths..." He trailed off, shaking his head. "You'll understand soon enough, Dr. Sharma. That's why you were summoned."

"What exactly did he leave me?"

"A box. Locked. He said only you could open it—that the key would know its owner." Jeremiah glanced back at her. "He also left a warning. The third night after your arrival, go to the east wing. The door will be open. But be careful what you find there, Dr. Sharma. The manor has a way of showing people what they're afraid to see."

Ravanhalli Manor rose before her like a fever dream—Victorian spires stabbing into the misty sky, windows dark and hollow.

---

## The Master of Ravanhalli

Thomas Ravanhalli stood in the doorway—fifty-eight, silver hair, face cold and calculating. "Dr. Sharma. Welcome to your family home."

In his study, Aisha found shelves lined with books in languages she didn't recognize—Sanskrit, Pali, something older. A fire burned, but the room felt cold.

"My brother Marcus is dead," Thomas said. "He died three months ago, under unusual circumstances. Before his death, he asked that you be contacted. He left you something—a box, containing his research. He believed you were the only one who could understand it."

"Why me? I barely knew the man."

"That's between you and Marcus." Thomas produced a small wooden box. "The key will only open for you."

Aisha held the key against the box's lock. Click. The lid swung open.

Inside lay a single journal, and a photograph. Aisha picked up the photograph, and her breath caught.

It was her mother. Younger than she'd ever known her, standing before Ravanhalli Manor, pregnant. And behind her, half-hidden by the mist, stood seven figures.

"The Seven," Thomas said quietly. "They vanished in 1894. Your mother was there when it happened."

Aisha looked up, her heart pounding. "What happened to them? What happened to my mother?"

"That's what Marcus was trying to discover. And now, Dr. Sharma, I believe it's your turn to find out."

At that moment, the lights flickered. And in the darkness, Aisha heard something—a whisper, forming words she almost understood.

*Welcome home, Aisha. Welcome home.*

---

## The Night Visitor

Aditi Ravanhalli appeared at Aisha's door that night—beautiful, with dark hair and darker eyes, trembling.

"You need to leave," the woman whispered. "Before the third night. Before the mist comes in."

"Who are you?"

"Aditi. Aditi Ravanhalli. And I know what you're looking for. But some answers are too dangerous to find." She pressed a folded note into Aisha's hand. "Read this. Then burn it. Please, Dr. Sharma—leave while you still can."

Inside the note, in a shaky hand, were written two words:

*THE WATCHER*
